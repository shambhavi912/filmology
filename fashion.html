Decoding every outfit on season 2 of Euphoria

Costume stylists Heidi Bivens and Angelina Vitto talk to Vogue about the characters on the show, the clothes that make them, and (wait for it) where to shop the looks BY DEVKI BHATT 27 JANUARY 2022

Streaming in India on Disney Hotstar, Sam Levison’s unsanitized story of American high schoolers narrated by Rue Bennett (played by Zendaya), Euphoria is creative storytelling at its best. A riveting story of teenagers navigating life, love, friendships, and addictions, the HBO series is a show that will have you rooting for its characters. With season two now three episodes down, Cal Jacobs gets his own story, Rue, Jules and Eliott (yup, you’ll get to this new addition soon enough), end up in a love triangle, and Nate finds himself drawn to an unlikely source of attraction, Cassie Howard. We will leave out the spoilers. What stands out strongly in the hyper-stylized visuals of Euphoria, are the second-skin-like costumes of the characters. Be it Jules Vaugh’s (played by Hunter Schafer) anime-esque self-exploratory style, Kat Hernandez’s (played by Barbie Ferriera) BDSM-inspired accessorizing, or Cassie Howard’s (played by Sydney Sweeney) mall-culture aesthetic, the costumes give a louder voice to the story each character holds. Through Emmy Award nominee costume stylist and director, Heidi Bivens’ creative vision, the show revolutionises the art of storytelling via fashion. Bivens’ groundbreaking work has appeared in The Beach Bum (2019), which has famously been called “the most important fashion film”, as well as Spring Breakers (2012) and Mid 90s (2018). Her styling, impulsive, raw, and with a strong note of escapism fills in the blanks of the protagonists' journeys as the show unravels. In an interview with the creative, she gives us insight into her process— “Sam, in the scripts, had written some clues to the characters’ socioeconomic backgrounds. Those helped me build my own backstories, which became the kind of filter I ran creative ideas through; how much is this character spending on clothes, what do their parents do for a living, what are they trying to express by the way they present themselves.” she explains. “Storytelling and world-building are what interest me the most. But I am also excited by a more abstract approach to the process,” says Bivens when asked about the relationship between fashion and film. “Things don't always have to make sense to evoke feelings from the audience. Ultimately in watching Euphoria, I think the whole team has created an opportunity for the viewer to feel something, whatever that may be,” she notes. In collaboration with her assistant stylist Angelina Vitto, Bivens started work for season 2 of Euphoria before the pandemic hit. Anyone with a keen fashion eye will note Bivens’ proclivity towards vintage and thrift. In sync, and working together like a well-oiled machine, Vitto and Bivens together pulled in and curated the looks for the new season. Vitto’s focus was to introduce a vast array of labels from Miu Miu to FashionNova, to which Bivens added her instinctive aesthetic of high-low fashion. Take for example, the look Bivens created for Cassie Howard in Prada heels and a House of CB dress. Or when she dressed Zendaya’s character, in a vintage thermal from a thrift store with a Jean Paul Gaultier vest on top. From the designers they worked with, to where to shop some of the looks, Bivens and Vitto decode the looks in season 2 of Euphoria:

Jules Vaughn (played by Hunter Schafer)

Bivens describes Jules’s style as “wildly expressive with a penchant for layering”. Her character is still exploring and figuring out how to be comfortable in her own skin. Vitto wanted to pull from the community of designers that resonate with this urge to explore her sexuality and self-expression. She notes, “The Women’s History Museum was a huge influence, while Cafe Forgot was a great resource for discovering new brands.” Jules’s character wears a lot of layering with a mix of sheer tops, box pleated skirts, and accessories like chokers, bulky skate shoes, and a bunny bag.

Rue Bennett (played by Zendaya)

“Rue isn't someone who thinks much about what she wears. A lot of her clothes are found; grabbed from a party or borrowed from friends and family,” says Vitto. With key terms being comfortable, gender fluid, functional with a play on proportions, Vitto added, “we slipped some fun stuff in there from Porcell and Varsity. However since Rue’s character deals with some pretty serious stuff in season 2, we wanted her to feel darker.” Her style comprises of micro tees, menswear inspired trousers, oversized skater T-shirts and her classic converse sneakers.

Cassie Howard (played by Sydney Sweeney)

Cassie Howard’s character simply put, is lost. In Vitto's words, “she dictates her style based on what she thinks men want and her innate need to be loved. It is very classic, standard femininity.” Dressing her in hyper feminine labels like Fleur du Mal and Agent Provocateur as well as floral dresses from Reformation, Vitto further played with the pin-up, mall-culture style of the character envisioned by Bivens. She also notes using Fiorucci and Bareminimale sweatsuits during the character’s depressive episodes.

Maddy Perez (played by Alexa Demie)

“I think using black on Maddy makes her seem tougher as her character has been through a lot. Heidi and I also talked a lot about how much Samantha [the woman who she babysits for, whose closet boasts of vintage Dior, Thierry Mugler Kessler, Halston and Chanel sourced from Aralda Vintage in Los Angeles] has influenced her. We wanted it to feel like she emulated her in a way,” recollects Vitto. She further adds, “Alexa Demie has amazing relationships with brands and brings a lot to the table.” While she worked with a plethora of brands, Vitto also notes resorting to eBay for items like True Religion jeans. In essence, Bivens characterized Maddy Perez’s style as fashion conscious and trend setting.

Kat Hernandez (played by Barbie Ferreira)

Kat Hernandez’s character played by Barbie Ferreira is possibly the most transformative of them all. Starting off with conservative collared tops and high waisted bottoms, Ferreira’s character evolves into a full on punk-subversive with plaid skirts, fishnets, harnesses worn over band T-shirts and lots of lace and leather. “In season 2 we want Kat to feel sexy but cool. We did bootleg Cramps shirt and cardigan to start her school looks. Definitely drawing inspiration from ‘90s Drew Barrymore.” says Vitto.
